ABOUT US

Dobrobat focuses on the initial reconstruction of damaged buildings. The project's priority is "fast recovery," or rapid restoration: re-roofing damaged roofs, repairing broken windows, and rebuilding damaged walls.

Volunteer centres are located in Kyiv, Chernihiv, Sumy, Kharkiv, Mykolaiv, Zaporizhzhia, Odesa and Kherson Oblasts

OUR MISSSION & GOALS

  • 01
    We want to help internally displaced people to return home and avoid becoming refugees
  • 02
    We want Connect global construction industry and global Ukraine supporters to the volunteers on the ground in Ukraine who restore damaged buildings and infrastructure
  • 03
    Restore 50,000 homes and 500 social infrastructure buildings (schools, kindergartens, urgent care facilities). We are >20% there now.
  • 04
    To inspire hope and resilience by bringing activities together to rebuild, recover and help as many ukrainians affected by the war stay home with the help of dedicated volunteers and partners

About:
IREX is an international nonprofit organization focused on education and development, partnering with Dobrobat to clear debris from Ukrainian homes through the "Ukrainian Rapid Response Fund."
Active in Ukraine since 1995, IREX supports displaced persons, veterans, and strengthens societal resilience by improving institutions, media literacy, and education.
About:
Open Door Ukraine (ODU) is a Dutch nonprofit organization that supports businesses, civil society, and other entities collaborating with Ukraine.
Established in 2019, ODU focuses on Ukraine's development, European integration, and restoring war-damaged infrastructure through intensified humanitarian efforts.
About:
Mondo is an Estonian charitable organization that supplies building materials for restoring damaged homes and social infrastructure, helping Dobrobat implement its projects.
Founded in 2007, Mondo operates in 13 countries, including Ukraine, focusing on education, healthcare, and humanitarian aid.
About:
Founded in Liechtenstein over 80 years ago, Hilti operates in 120+ countries, offering innovative equipment, software, and services for safer, more sustainable construction.
With its own R&D labs and global partnerships, Hilti contributes to restoring Ukraine's infrastructure.
About:
Nova Ukraine is a nonprofit organization providing humanitarian aid and raising global awareness about Ukraine, supporting social infrastructure restoration through its partnership with Dobrobat.
Since 2014, the organization has delivered over $100 million in aid, working with local groups, hospitals, and authorities to promote cultural growth and social mobility.
About:
Škoda is a reliable partner of Dobrobat, ensuring efficient transportation of volunteers and materials, and facilitating meetings with partners and donors.
Founded in 1885 and part of Volkswagen AG since 1991, Škoda has a strong history of charitable initiatives in Ukraine.
About:
The partnership between Dobrobat and Baumit, a leading European construction company, has quickly led to numerous projects restoring war-damaged facilities across Ukraine. Founded in 1810, Baumit is known for innovations in construction materials, including the self-cleaning Baumit Nanopor plaster, and has contributed to Ukraine’s recovery by donating proceeds from their products sold since July 2024.
